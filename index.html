the art and craft of poetry

There is something magical about poetry, about the way lines of words can reveal truths, bring beauty, and generate tears. At the forefront, creativity, imagination, and maybe even a little bit of magic are certainly essential parts of creative writing, but there is a side to this craft — a meticulous and systematic side — that is seldom acknowledged.

In reality, the process of research — the gathering of data, the recording of critical information, and the analysis and interpretation of material — supports, guides, and ignites creative writing.

And then there is the study and skill of how to build a poem: how many lines to put in each stanza, where to put each line break, how to create different effects by long or short lines.

The words and meanings of poems are always in the spotlight. This project shines light on the elements of poetry that are often forgotten in the background.

exploring the poems

My chapbook, Hurricane Season, explores and concentrates on my connection with the ocean, the existence and essence of God, my wavering faith in religion, and, at times, my anger at the world.

As you journey to each page, you can interact with the poems in ways that shed light on how the pieces came into life, or that emphasize the form of the pieces, or that demonstrate how the art of the words can continue to be used to make new art on the screen.

You can explore the research behind the writing, you can look at the shape of the poem and the length of individual words, and you can even create waves out of the lines.

Poetry is art. But the art doesn’t come only from the meaning of the words on the page.
